The Me Too (or #MeToo) movement, a campaign which encourages women to talk about the sexual harassment and sexual assault that they have been subjected too, has caused a tremendous shock. This campaign, which was launched by women in prominent positions in the media, political, economic and even educational societies, has dispelled the three most important misconceptions that have fooled many people. All these misconceptions have, in a moment, been shattered and it has become clear that no one can possibly go against the pure innate nature God created us with, regarding attraction to the other sex.

The First

Misconception

Modern man has reached such a high degree of civilization and education that he has got rid of his primitive instincts and has become able to control his whims and desires.

The truth is that attraction to the opposite sex is the way God created us; which is the innate human instinct He has created for the survival of the human species. This essentially has to do with genes, hormones and nature and not with the level of one’s learning and modernisation.

Mankind’s manners, religion and morals are the values that efficiently serve to strengthen his determination and will and to control his whims and desires. However, this is a relative matter and depends on internal and external factors.

The Second

Misconception

Sexual harassment and sexual assault take place only in closed and strict societies. As for free and open societies, they are well-fortified against such crimes, with rare exceptions of course..

Well, the reverse is true. Statistics and studies on sexual harassment and rape prove this misconception wrong, stating that the highest-ranking countries in personal freedoms have the highest rate of sexual harassment and rape. It is true that deterrence and self-immunity are weakened in strict societies because the will is rarely tested in them; however, no matter how strong one’s will and immunity may be, they are prone to give, in open societies that are devoid of moral values, as all aspects of life around one contribute to destroy one’s will.

The Third

Misconception

The wide array of laws to protect women and the fleet of surveillance cameras that have filled modern cities are sufficient to deter these crimes and harassments.

Well, this is only part of the truth. Law and surveillance help deter crime and harassment, but we should not be content with them in other aspects of life. Indeed, the most efficient means of combatting crime always lies in detecting and eradicating its causes and motives. It is a truism that preventing the situation, that could cause a disease, is more efficient than restricting ourselves to treating this disease after its occurrence. Similarly, getting rid of stagnant, dirty water is undeniably more effective in curing malaria than simply dispensing medicines for it.

In this way, we fully understand Islamic legislation, which is based on the principle, “Prevention is better than cure”, when it lays down rules to preserve women’s goals and dignity and safeguard the beauty and flexibility of life. Just as it commands women to wear the hijab, it requires men to lower their gaze and not stare at women’s charms. It also warns them against meeting with women in an enclosed space without a third party. It isn’t surprising that these are the same rules set out by Wall Street businessmen today following the recent sexual harassment scandals. (See Mike Bloomberg).
